## Executive Summary

Our analysis of 148 employees across 10 departments reveals a company-wide attrition rate of **26.4%**, with **39 employees having left the organization**. The annual cost of this turnover is estimated at **$3.5M** in recruiting, training, and productivity losses, with the **Sales department alone accounting for $1.3M (37%)**. The organization's **Workforce Health Score is 56/100** — classified as "At Risk" — indicating systemic challenges in satisfaction, engagement, work-life balance, and management effectiveness. This report outlines 5 strategic priorities to reduce voluntary attrition by 15-20% and save approximately **$694K annually**.

## Department-Specific Recommendations

### Sales (39.3% Attrition)
- Immediate: Cap overtime at 10 hrs/week
- Short-term: Revise comp plan with quarterly bonuses
- Long-term: Create sales career tracks (Jr. → Sr. → Lead → Manager)

### Operations (33.3% Attrition, 50% Overtime)
- Redesign shift scheduling to reduce overtime
- Launch engagement initiatives (monthly team events)
- Improve promotion rate from 5.6% to 15%

### IT (31.8% Attrition)
- Review market competitiveness of salaries
- Create technical career ladder (IC path)
- Increase training and certification budgets
