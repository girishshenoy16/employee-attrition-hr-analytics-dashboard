# Project Overview

## What is This Project?

The **Employee Attrition & HR Analytics Dashboard** is a comprehensive data analytics project that analyzes employee turnover patterns within an organization. It combines Python-based data analysis with an interactive web dashboard to provide HR leaders and business stakeholders with actionable insights about their workforce.

## Why is Employee Attrition Analysis Important?

- **Cost Impact**: Replacing an employee costs 50-200% of their annual salary (recruiting, training, lost productivity)
- **Knowledge Loss**: Departing employees take institutional knowledge, client relationships, and team expertise
- **Morale Impact**: High attrition creates instability, increases workload on remaining staff, and lowers morale
- **Business Performance**: Companies with low attrition consistently outperform those with high turnover
- **Strategic Planning**: Understanding attrition drivers enables proactive retention strategies

## How Companies Use HR Analytics Dashboards

| Use Case | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Monitor Attrition Trends** | Track monthly/quarterly attrition rates across departments |
| **Identify Risk Factors** | Detect patterns around overtime, satisfaction, promotion gaps |
| **Segment Workforce** | Group employees by risk profiles for targeted interventions |
| **Measure Impact** | Quantify the effect of retention programs and policy changes |
| **Forecast Hiring** | Predict future attrition to plan recruitment needs |
| **Report to Leadership** | Present data-driven workforce insights to C-suite |

## Which Job Roles Use Such Systems

- **Business Analyst** — Analyzes data, builds dashboards, presents insights
- **HR Analyst** — Monitors workforce metrics, supports HR decision-making
- **People Analytics Specialist** — Deep-dives into employee data for strategic insights
- **HR Manager** — Uses dashboards for team management and retention planning
- **Workforce Planning Analyst** — Forecasts hiring needs based on attrition patterns
- **Talent Acquisition Specialist** — Plans recruitment pipelines from attrition data
- **CHRO / VP of HR** — Reviews high-level workforce health metrics
