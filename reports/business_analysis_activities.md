# Business Analysis Activities

## 1. Workforce Analysis

Analyze the composition of the workforce: headcount by department, age distribution, gender diversity, education levels, and tenure patterns. This provides a baseline understanding of the organization's human capital.

## 2. Attrition Analysis

Calculate and compare attrition rates across all employee segments. Identify departments, roles, and demographics with disproportionately high turnover. Analyze attrition reasons to understand root causes.

## 3. Employee Segmentation

Divide the workforce into meaningful groups based on shared characteristics (age bands, tenure brackets, satisfaction levels, performance tiers). This enables targeted analysis and intervention strategies.

## 4. Employee Engagement Analysis

Measure and compare engagement scores across the organization. Identify departments or roles with low engagement, which is a leading indicator of future attrition risk.

## 5. Retention Analysis

Analyze what keeps employees at the organization. Identify departments and employee segments with highest retention rates, and understand the factors that contribute to employee loyalty.

## 6. Root Cause Analysis

Using correlation analysis and cross-tabulation, identify the underlying drivers of attrition. Key questions:
- Do overtime workers leave at higher rates?
- Does lack of promotion lead to attrition?
- What satisfaction threshold predicts departure?
- How does manager rating affect retention?

## 7. Workforce Planning

Use attrition insights to forecast future hiring needs, plan succession pipelines, and develop retention strategies that reduce the need for reactive hiring.

## 8. HR KPI Analysis

Track and analyze key HR metrics against industry benchmarks. Monitor trends over time and identify areas requiring management attention.
