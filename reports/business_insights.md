# Business Insights

Based on the analysis of 148 employee records across 10 departments, the following key insights were derived:

## Insight 1: Attrition Rate Overview
- **Overall Attrition Rate**: 26.4% (39 of 148 employees)
- **Retention Rate**: 73.6%
- **Annual Attrition Cost**: $3.5M (Sales alone accounts for $1.3M)
- This indicates a significant turnover problem requiring immediate management attention

## Insight 2: Department with Highest Attrition
- **Sales** department has the highest attrition at 39.3%
- **Operations** follows at 33.3%, then **IT** at 31.8%
- **Finance** has 0% attrition — best retention in the company
- Sales attrition is 2.5x the Finance rate
- **Highest-risk department** (by risk score): **Operations** (14.4 avg)

## Insight 3: Workforce Health Score
- **Workforce Health Score**: 56/100 — classified as **"At Risk"**
- Composite metric based on satisfaction, engagement, work-life balance, and manager ratings
- Below the 60-point threshold that indicates a healthy workforce
- Raising to 70+ could reduce attrition risk significantly

## Insight 4: Attrition Reasons
- **Work-Life Balance** (28.2%) is the #1 reason employees leave
- **Career Growth** (20.5%) is the #2 reason
- **Management** (17.9%) is #3
- Compensation is only the 5th most cited reason (7.7%)

## Insight 5: Overtime Impact
- Employees working overtime have **37.5% attrition** vs **21.0%** for non-overtime employees
- Overtime workers are **1.8x more likely** to leave
- Legal (60%) and Operations (50%) have highest overtime rates

## Insight 6: Satisfaction & Engagement
- **Average satisfaction**: 3.4/5 (moderate)
- IT department has highest satisfaction (3.77)
- Operations has lowest engagement (2.78)
- Employees with satisfaction <= 2 have significantly higher attrition

## Insight 7: Promotion & Attrition
- **Promotion rate**: 18.2% company-wide
- Employees not promoted have **28.1% attrition** vs **18.5%** for promoted employees
- 40.9% of IT department was promoted (highest) vs 5.6% in Operations (lowest)

## Insight 8: Risk Score Distribution
- **Critical** (> 18): 8 employees (5.4%) — immediate intervention required
- **High** (11–18): 74 employees (50.0%) — monitor and engage
- **Medium** (9–11): 41 employees (27.7%) — standard retention programs
- **Low** (≤ 9): 25 employees (16.9%) — minimal risk
- Risk threshold for department table: > 13 Critical, 11–13 High, 9–11 Medium, < 9 Low

## Insight 9: Correlation Analysis
- **Risk Score** has the strongest composite correlation with attrition
- **Job Satisfaction** (r = -0.21) and **Work-Life Balance** (r = -0.19) have strongest negative correlation
- Engagement score alone is a moderate predictor; combined with satisfaction it becomes powerful

## Insight 10: Demographic Patterns
- **Age**: Attrition is highest in the 40-49 age band (30%) and lowest in 60+ (19%)
- **Marital Status**: Married employees have higher attrition (31.9%) than single (25%) or divorced (10.5%)
- **Gender**: Male attrition (28.2%) is slightly higher than Female (22.6%)

## Insight 11: At-Risk Employee Profile
The typical high-risk employee:
- Works in Sales, Operations, or Support
- Has satisfaction score <= 2
- Works overtime regularly
- Hasn't been promoted in 3+ years
- Shows low engagement (< 3)

## Insight 12: Retention Opportunity
The organization has several high-performing employees with low satisfaction who have not been promoted — **23 high-performers (rating ≥ 4) with no promotion in 2+ years**. These represent both the biggest flight risk and the biggest retention opportunity. Targeted retention programs could reduce voluntary turnover by 15-20%, saving approximately **$694K annually**.

## Insight 13: Retention Forecast
Under current trends, retention is projected to decline further. A 20% reduction in attrition through targeted interventions could save $694K and stabilize the workforce at 80%+ retention within 12 months.
