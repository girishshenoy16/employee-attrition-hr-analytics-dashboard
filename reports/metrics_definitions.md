# HR Metrics Definitions

## 1. Attrition Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Number of Employees Who Left / Total Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Percentage of workforce that left during the period. Higher values indicate retention problems.

## 2. Retention Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Number of Employees Who Stayed / Total Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Percentage of workforce retained. The inverse of attrition rate.

## 3. Average Tenure

**Formula**: Sum(Years at Company) / Total Employees
**Interpretation**: Average number of years employees spend at the organization.

## 4. Average Monthly Income

**Formula**: Sum(Monthly Income) / Total Employees
**Interpretation**: Average salary across the workforce. Useful for comparing compensation fairness.

## 5. Average Satisfaction Score

**Formula**: Sum(Job Satisfaction Score) / Total Employees
**Scale**: 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High)
**Interpretation**: Overall employee satisfaction level. Scores below 3 indicate potential issues.

## 6. Average Engagement Score

**Formula**: Sum(Engagement Score) / Total Employees
**Scale**: 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High)
**Interpretation**: Employee commitment and involvement level. Leading indicator for attrition.

## 7. Promotion Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Employees Promoted / Total Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Percentage of workforce receiving promotions. Low rates may drive attrition.

## 8. Overtime Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Employees Working Overtime / Total Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Percentage of workforce on overtime. High rates correlate with burnout and attrition.

## 9. Training Participation Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Employees with Training Hours > 0 / Total Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Percentage of employees participating in training. Indicator of development investment.

## 10. Department-wise Attrition Rate (%)

**Formula**: (Employees Left in Department / Total Department Employees) x 100
**Interpretation**: Attrition rate specific to each department. Used to identify problem areas.
